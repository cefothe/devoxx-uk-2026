# Speaker Bio

## Long Version (≈110 words — programme committee, talk page)

Stefan Angelov is an Engineering Manager at Tradu and a seasoned Software Architect with over 10 years of experience building high-performance, scalable systems in the fintech and gaming industries. He has held architecture and leadership roles designing distributed systems handling millions of transactions.

Stefan is passionate about Java, Spring Boot, Apache Kafka, and cloud-native architectures on AWS. Beyond his day job, he co-founded Hacker4e, an academy teaching kids to code through Scratch and web development — proof that he believes great software starts with great fundamentals.

A member of the FinOps Foundation community and a regular on the tech conference circuit, Stefan enjoys sharing hard-won lessons on system design, event-driven architecture, and engineering leadership.

## Notes for the Programme Committee

- Stefan has not previously spoken at Devoxx UK. He has published in JavaPro (Issue 05/2025) and is a regular on the tech conference circuit.
- The talk's headline production numbers (5M ops/sec, 0.5µs median, 42µs p99 on a 2014 Xeon) come from low-latency trading systems Stefan has worked on across his career. The open-source `exchange-core` project is cited as an independent, public proof point — anyone in the room can clone and reproduce it.
- Industry scope: Stefan's career spans fintech (multi-asset trading, CFDs/equities, crypto-adjacent platforms) and gaming. The talk's techniques apply to all of them — they are about the JVM and the hardware, not about any one asset class.
